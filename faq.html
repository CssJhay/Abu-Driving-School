FAQS

  • What's the process for scheduling a lesson?

    To book a lesson, simply submit a request online, and we'll promptly reach out to you to gather more information and finalize the booking

  • How many lessons should I plan for my training?

    The number of lessons needed for your training can vary depending on your individual skill level and the assessment conducted by your instructor during your first lesson. Your instructor will assess your current proficiency and provide you with a more accurate estimate of the number of lessons required to meet your goals.

  • What payment methods are available for lessons?

    We offer several convenient payment options, including cash, email transfer, credit card, and virtual currency, such as ETH. Additionally, we provide various package deals designed to help you save money on your lessons.

  • How can I book a written or road test?

    To schedule your written or road test, you have a few options. You can visit an MPI office or an Autopac location in person. Alternatively, you can conveniently book your test online by visiting the MPI website at https://www.mpi.mb.ca/Pages/Online-Services.aspx

  • What is the waiting period for taking the road test after passing the written test?

    The waiting period for taking the road test depends on your prior licensing status. If you've never held a driver's license before, you must wait for 9 months before being eligible for the road test. However, if you currently hold a valid driver's license from another jurisdiction, you may be eligible to apply for a permanent license without waiting.

  • If I hold a Learner's license from another Canadian province, will I need to retake the written test when I move to Manitoba?

    No, you will not be able to transfer your Learner's license from another province to Manitoba. MPI does not recognize Learner's licenses from other provinces, so you will need to follow the licensing process in Manitoba, which may involve retaking the written test.
